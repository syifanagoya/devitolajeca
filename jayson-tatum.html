Jayson Tatum | Professional Career In Boston Celtics

Boston Celtics is a professional basketball team that competes for the NBA as a member of the league’s Eastern Conference Atlantic Division.

Boston Celtics drafted Jayson as the #3 overall on June 22, 2017, even though he was not superiorly ranked like other candidates picked by Philadelphia.

The Boston Celtics basketball operations president, Danny Ainge, counted Jayson Tatum instead of signing top NBA players, contrary to their rivals Philadelphia 76ers. 

When Tatum Was NBA’s Newbie

Jayson Tatum gained a double-double with 14 points and ten rebounds as the team’s starting power forward in his NBA debut. However, his team had to undergo a loss to the Cleveland Cavaliers.

Tatum proved to the crowd how capable he was in his rookie season. He scored 18.7 points, 9.7 rebounds, 2.3 steals, and 2.0 assists in an average within 33 minutes of action at the 2017 NBA Summer League event in Utah.

Similarly, he gained 17.7 points, 8.0 rebounds, 1.0 assists, and 0.8 blocks in an average of 32 minutes at Las Vegas in the three games he participated in.

Thereupon, Jayson was named to All-summer League Second Team alongside Bryn Forbes, Cheick Diallo, Wayne Selden Jr., and Kyle Kuzma. 

Tatum attained a season-high 24 points over the New York Knights on October 24, 2017. Henceforth, he was honored with Eastern Conference’s Rookie of The Month for the December 2017 title.

The Boston Celtics ended the season with 55-27, paving their path toward the 2018 NBA Playoffs as the second seed in the Eastern Conference.

Tatum grabbed a double-double with 19 points and ten rebounds in Game 1 of the first-round series against the seventh seed, Milwaukee Bucks. 

He subsequently broke his playoff-high record twice. First, in Game 4 with 21 points, then in Game 6 with 22 points. Moreover, the Boston Celtics defeated the Milwaukee Bucks in Game 7 with a 112-96 win. 

Records Made By Tatum In His Rookie Year

Jayson Tatum scored a career-high 28 points in a 117–101 victory against the third-seed Philadelphia 76ers in Game 1 of the second-round series. 

This record established him as the first Boston Celtics rookie to score 25 or more points in a playoff game since Larry Bird during the 1980 NBA Playoffs. Both Tatum and Bird scored a point against the 76ers.

Furthermore, he scored 21 points in a Game 2 victory against the Boston Celtics.

Tatum was the youngest player to attain at least 20 points in four straight playoff games at 20 years and 68 days, even earlier than his idol, Kobe Bryant.

Kobe Bryant scored the same number during the 1999 NBA Playoffs when he was 20 years and 272 days old. 

Moreover, he contributed to giving a Game 3 overtime win for Philadelphia to his team by scoring 24 points.

Then, Tatum stood up as the first Celtics rookie to score 20 points in five straight playoff games. Larry Bird held the previous record of 20 points in five straight playoff games.

He joined Kareem Abdul and Jabbar on the squad of rookies in playoff history to record ten games of 20 or more points scored during their first playoff runs.

Jayson Tatum was named to the NBA All-Rookie First Team on May 22, 2018.
